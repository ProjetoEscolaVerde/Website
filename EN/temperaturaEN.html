The sensor


All data on the ambient temperature were collected from the interior of the greenhouse. The sensor we are using is the DHT22 ,                which has a decimal point accuracy for both temperature and atmospheric humidity.

TIPOS DE GRÁFICOS

Daily

The data are collected every exact 30 minutes in the interval closed from 00h00m to 23h30m. The first two charts fit into this category.

Monthly

The temperature in each day is calculated by the average of the 48 values collected on the respective day (24 hours).

Yearly

Divided in weeks - average temperature of 7 days - and shows all the data collected from the beginning of the collections. We consider the first week of each month as the first week started on Sunday in a given month.

About temperature

Every body has temperature and it can be defined as the average kinetic energy of the molecules of this body. As in the vacuum                 there is no matter, there is no temperature.

In addition, the concept of cold and warm is variable and depends on the temperature difference between                 the bodies that are being analyzed, since the heat is transferred naturamente of the body of greater temperature to the one of smaller.               When we lose heat to another body, we feel the sensation of cold; this is because body temperature was higher             higher than the temperature of the other body. A body at 250 ° C can be considered cold to another body at 350 ° C.

In order to establish a way of measuring temperature, mankind has created different temperature scales. Between them:               celcius, fahrenheit and kelvin. In order to define 0 of the celcius scale, the melting point of the water at sea level was used.               On the Kelvin scale, temperature 0 is the temperature at which matter stops vibrating. In the fahrenheit scale, temperature 0 was               defined by a given column of mercury in a solution of water, ice and ammonium salt, and temperature 100 has been defined                 by the height of the same column of mercury in the human body. (note: although there is degree celcius (ºC) and degree fahrenheit (ºF), there is no kelvin degree (K))

Use in class

Annual change

From the annual graph of annual temperature variation it is possible to observe how the average temperature decreases and increases in the course                        of the year. From this analysis it is possible to discuss how the different seasons affect the average temperature. Besides that,                        it is possible to correlate with the translation movement of the Earth around the Sun and the different angles of inclination of the rays                        over the earth's surface.

Daily change

From the chart "Yesterday" and "Today" it is possible to analyze the variation of the temperature in the period of one day. This phenomenon                        can initiate discussions about the earth's rotational motion and the degree of inclination of the sun's rays. Besides that,                       it is possible to discuss the phenomenon of energy transport by irradiation from the Sun to Earth and its conversion to thermal energy                       when reaching the earth's surface.

Temperature in greenhouses

From the presentation of the temperature in a greenhouse, it is possible to discuss about the increase of the wavelength                      when the electromagnetic rays hit the ground and are reflected as infrared light, which prevents some rays from leaving                    of the greenhouse and as a consequence make the environment inside the greenhouse with a higher temperature than the outside. It's important to highlight                  that the same effect of temperature increase occurs on a global scale naturally by the "greenhouse effect", but is aggravated by the                  release of CO2 and CH4 (which prevents less energetic electromagnetic waves from escaping from the earth).
