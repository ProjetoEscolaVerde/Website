The Escol@Verde project

The Beginning

The Escol@Verde project is a follow-up to a interdisciplinary school activite (Projeto Energia) about the theme of Sustentable Energy Sources, done in Colégio Cândido Portinari, focusing on the junior high school students of 2017. The work made by the team consisting of eleven students was a portable and automatic greenhouse, with a solar power supply. Of those eleven students, six decided to continue the research on the matter, not long after they had received a positive feedback from the teachers. The idea for the project Escol@ Verde was an concept tackle by the Robotics teacher, Fábio Ferreira, the advisor. In his vision, the team had demostrated a capacity to extend the project even futher.

The Escol@Verde

Learning isn't only taught inside the walls of a classroom. The disciplining of knowledge fragments one's understanding of the subject at hand, however, when this knowledge is reproduced in natural and social phenomenons, it is desfragmented. The teaching and learning process needs to reconect the knowledgment, enabling students to see the bigger picture, and see things with a global vision. Interdisciplinarity activites and transinterdisplinarity are important and must be applyed in the school environment, as it lets students comprehend the world as an whole, with it's own complexity. The Escol@Verde has the goal to instigate interdisplinarity, transdisplinarity and disciplinarity activites in the school environment with practical classes, that also estimulate resarching, learning and acquaintanceship inside the school. Applying the acquired knowledge and narrowing the relationship within the school community also permeates the proposal of a school that enhances students's skills and abilities. The Escol@Verde project proposes a scientific, pedagogical and social environment pinpointed by a automatic and self-sustantining greenhouse. The Escol@Verde, a project developed in Colégio Cândido Portinari, forms the tecnological structure to a school that is open to rethink and to reconstrute itself. All the data colected by sensors and actions controls are shown by this site via IoT (Internet of Things), making the information accessible at any time and anywhere, not only for the teachers but for the students. Futhermore, the greenhouse has it's own automatic irrigation and water control system, making it possible to cultivate inside up to 7 sectors of plantation with different irrigation configurations. The greenhouse isn't dependent of the traditional energy sources, as a result of having it's own power system using solar energy converted into electric energy (clean and renewable energy) that is stored in a stationary battery for the use of the greenhouse itself, which will power the microcontroller, sensors and effectors.
